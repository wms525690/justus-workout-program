The Big 3 Checklist

Master these three fundamentals. Every session starts here.

👣

Foot Position / Mechanics

Short Foot Activation → Balance Drill

3 sets · Build the arch seated, then stand and hold single-leg · Log time per set

Cue: Pull ball of foot toward heel without curling toes -- build the arch. Once you feel it, stand on that foot. Let the arch engagement gently spiral your knee and femur outward into external rotation. This is the alignment that protects the ACL. Hold as long as you can maintain the arch and the rotation.

Toe Yoga (Splay & Isolate)

2 sets · 8 reps · Big toe up / little toes down, then switch

Cue: Control each toe independently. Slow is the goal.

▶ Watch video

Ball-of-Foot Balance (Short Foot + ER)

3 sets each foot · Log time per set

Cue: Rise onto the balls of your feet, heels off the ground. Maintain the short foot arch -- don't let it collapse. Feel the knee and femur gently rotate outward from the foot's position. The foot dictates whether the knee is protected or compromised. Hold as long as form holds.

🧬

Bracing / Breathing

360 Breathing

3 sets · 8 breaths per set

Cue: Seated or supine. Inhale through the nose and expand into ALL directions -- front, sides, and back. Ribs should expand laterally, not rise up. Exhale fully and feel the ribs button down toward the belly button. No chest rise. This is your brace foundation.

▶ Watch video

Dead Bug (Static → Movement)

4 sets · Set 1: static hold, lock position · Sets 2-4: slow contralateral movement · Log reps per set

Cue: Ribs buttoned down, low back flat on the floor. Set 1 -- just hold the position and breathe through your brace. Own it before you move. Sets 2-4 -- slow opposite arm and leg extension. The moment your back arches off the floor, the set is over. Initiate every rep from the exhale, from the core out.

▶ Watch video

Bear Crawl (Static Hold → Crawl)

4 sets · Set 1: static hold, knees 1" off ground · Sets 2-4: slow controlled crawling · Log time per set

Cue: Set 1 -- tuck toes, lift knees 1 inch. Hold. Breathe through the brace. If you're shaking, good. Sets 2-4 -- opposite hand and foot move together, slow and controlled. A cup of water on your back should not spill. Synchronize the breath with each step. Nasal breathing only -- if you have to mouth-breathe, slow down.

Neck / Shoulder Position

Chin Tuck (Deep Neck Flexor Activation)

3 sets · 10 reps · 5s hold

Cue: Make a double chin. Pull the back of your skull straight back. Keep eyes level.

Wall Slide (Scapular Upward Rotation)

2 sets · 10 reps

Cue: Back flat on wall, chin tucked, eyes level. Elbows and wrists stay in contact. Slide up slow, squeeze at top. Maintain the chin tuck the entire time -- if the head pushes forward, you've lost the neck position.

02

Mobility

CARS, active end-range strength, and balance integration. Ground up, joint by joint. Move slow, breathe through it, reach your absolute max range.

🦶

Ankle

Ankle CARS (Supine → Standing Balance)

4 sets each ankle · Sets 1-3: supine, knee pulled to chest · Set 4: standing on opposite leg

Cue: Full circumduction in both directions -- reach your absolute maximum range at every point in the circle. Slow and controlled. Some cramping is normal, that's the edge. Sub-max breathing throughout. On set 4, standing version -- short foot arch on the plant leg, knee/femur externally rotated. The balance IS the progression.

▶ Watch video

Deep Squat Soleus Calf Raises

3 sets · Log reps per set

Cue: Sit into a deep squat, feet flat. From the bottom, drive up onto the balls of your feet (plantar flexion), then press heels back down past flat (dorsiflexion focus). Keep your chest tall and ribs buttoned down. The squat position isolates the soleus and challenges ankle ROM under load.

▶ Watch video

Squat to Hamstring Stretch (Flat + Heel Elevated)

3 sets · Sets 1-2: feet flat · Set 3: heels elevated · Log reps per set

Cue: Drop into a deep squat, grab your toes. Straighten the legs to stretch the hamstrings while holding your toes, then pull yourself back down into the squat. Feet flat first to challenge ankle dorsiflexion. Heel elevated variation lets you go deeper into the hamstring stretch. Exhale as you extend. Ribs down throughout.

▶ Watch video
🚴

Hip

Hip CARS (Quadruped → Standing Balance)

4 sets each hip · Sets 1-3: quadruped · Set 4: standing on opposite leg

Cue: Brace everything except the working hip. Lift knee up, rotate it out as wide as possible, extend behind you, reverse. The biggest, slowest circle you can make. Zero compensation at the pelvis or spine. On set 4, stand on the opposite leg -- short foot, knee externally rotated. The standing version exposes every weakness. Sub-max breathing throughout.

▶ Watch video

90/90 Passive Stretch → Active Lift-Offs

3 sets each side · Set 1: passive hold, sink into the stretch · Sets 2-3: active lift-offs · Log reps per set

Cue: Set 1 -- sit in the 90/90, chest tall, breathe into the stretch. Own the position. Sets 2-3 -- lift the back knee 1-2 inches off the floor using only hip internal rotation, then lift the front knee using hip external rotation. These are humbling. Small movement is fine. Initiate from the breath out.

▶ Watch video

Cossack Squat

3 sets · Log reps per set (alternating sides)

Cue: Shift laterally into a deep side lunge. Sit as low as possible over the bent leg -- the straight leg stays extended with toes up. Drive through the bent leg to return. Chest tall, ribs down. Short foot on the working leg. Feel the deep hip and adductor stretch on the straight side. Control the bottom position -- don't bounce.

▶ Watch video

Kneeling Hip Flexions to Kicks (Supported)

3 sets each leg · Log reps per set

Cue: Start double-kneeling, holding PVC sticks or a wall for balance. Drive one knee into maximal hip flexion -- as high as you can. Alternate legs. Progression: from that top position, extend the knee fully and kick the leg straight out (high knee to full kick). Ribs down, brace through the movement. The support lets you focus on max range, not balance. Exhale on the drive up.

▶ Watch video

Deep Squat Knee Adduction / Abduction Isometrics

3 sets · 10s squeeze each direction per set

Cue: Sit in a deep squat, elbows inside your knees. Adduction -- squeeze your knees hard against your elbows for 10 seconds. Abduction -- press your elbows against your knees and drive knees outward for 10 seconds, stretching the groin. Breathe through both. Ribs down, chest tall. These build end-range strength where you're most vulnerable.

▶ Watch video
⚖️

Low Back

Prone Back Extensions → Full Back + Hip Extension

3 sets · Set 1: chest lift only · Sets 2-3: chest AND legs lift simultaneously · Log reps or time per set

Cue: Face down, arms by your sides or overhead. Set 1 -- lift only the chest off the ground, squeeze the upper back. Sets 2-3 -- lift the chest AND the legs simultaneously, creating a full extension from head to toe. Squeeze glutes hard. Breathe -- don't hold your breath. Control the lowering.

▶ Watch video

Prone Alternating Heel Touches

3 sets · Log reps per set (alternating sides)

Cue: Face down, chest and legs lifted off the ground. Right hand reaches back and across to touch the left foot as it extends back to meet the hand in the middle of the back. Alternate sides. This demands rotation, extension, and coordination through the entire posterior chain. Breathe -- exhale as you reach.

▶ Watch video

Marinovich Swiss Ball Side-to-Side Rotations

3 sets · Log reps per set (total rotations)

Cue: Hands firmly on the ground, knees together on a Swiss ball. Roll the ball side to side by rotating your lower body while keeping your hands planted and shoulders stable. Knees stay together. This trains core and lower back rotational control, rooted in shoulder stability. Slow and controlled -- the ball exposes every compensation. Sub-max breathing throughout.

▶ Watch video
💪

Shoulder

Prone Shoulder CARS (Handcuff Position)

3 sets each arm

Cue: Face down, start with hands behind the back like handcuffs. Sweep the arm through a full shoulder ROM -- out to the side, overhead into the Y position, and back. Emphasize HEIGHT off the ground at every position in the arc. Use objects (water bottles in the overhead position) to create accountability -- you must clear them. Slow, controlled, max range. Cramping is normal at the edges. Sub-max breathing.

▶ Watch video

Prone W to Y Overhead Press

3 sets · Log reps per set

Cue: Face down, arms in the W position (elbows bent, externally rotated). Press the arms from W to full Y overhead, maintaining height off the ground the entire time. Squeeze the lower traps at the top of the Y. Control the return back to W. No shrugging -- if your upper traps fire first, reduce the range. This builds end-range overhead strength.

▶ Watch video

Bridge to L-Sit

3 sets · Log reps per set

Cue: Start in an L-sit -- legs extended, hands planted by your hips. Press heels into the ground and drive hips and chest up high into a bridge (full shoulder extension). Sit hips back down and THROUGH your hands, back to L-sit, keeping your hips off the ground the entire time. This demands shoulder extension, thoracic mobility, and full-body control. Exhale on the drive up.

▶ Watch video

Scapula Push-Ups

3 sets · Log reps per set

Cue: Push-up position, arms locked out. Without bending the elbows, let your chest sink between your shoulder blades (retraction), then push the ground away as hard as you can, rounding the upper back (protraction). This is pure scapular movement -- the serratus anterior drives the protraction. Ribs down, core braced. If your low back sags, the scapula can't move cleanly. Sub-max breathing throughout.

▶ Watch video

Pull-Up Bar Hang → Scapular Pull-Ups → Simple Planche

3 sets · Set 1: passive dead hang (log time) · Set 2: scapular pull-ups (log reps) · Set 3: simple planche hold (log time)

Cue: Set 1 -- dead hang, let gravity decompress the shoulders. Relax everything except the grip. Breathe. Set 2 -- from the hang, pull your shoulder blades down and together without bending the elbows. This is scapular depression and retraction under load. Set 3 -- lean forward in the hang, shifting weight over the hands toward a planche position. Hold what you can. These progress shoulder stability from passive to extremely demanding.

Wrist / Forearm

Wrist CARS + Pronation / Supination

3 sets each wrist · Full circumduction + pronation/supination

Cue: Make a fist. Slow, controlled circumduction in both directions -- reach max range at every point. Then pronation/supination: rotate the forearm palm-up to palm-down through full range. Relevant for grip strength and throwing mechanics. Can be done standing on one leg as a balance add.

▶ Watch video

Passive Wrist Stretches (Flexion / Extension / Prayer)

2 sets · 20-30s each position

Cue: Extension -- palm down on a surface, lean forward to stretch the wrist into extension. Flexion -- back of hand on a surface, lean forward. Prayer -- palms together at chest height, press hands down while keeping palms in contact. Hold each and breathe. These maintain the wrist mobility needed for bear crawl work, push-ups, and catching.

😄

Neck

Neck CARS (Quadruped + Supine Off-Bench)

3 sets · Sets 1-2: quadruped · Set 3: supine with head hanging off bench/bed

Cue: Full cervical circumduction -- slow, controlled, max range in both directions. Quadruped: maintain chin tuck as your baseline, then move through the full circle. Supine off-bench: head hangs off the edge, move through full flexion, extension, lateral flexion, and rotation. The unsupported position exposes the full range. Maintain sub-max breathing -- especially here. Eyes stay level when possible. Chin tuck is the home base you return to.

Arm Care

Protect the arm. Rotator cuff health, scapular control, and throwing longevity. Rib cage position drives scapular mechanics -- if the ribs flare, the scapula can't sit on the cage properly, and the shoulder pays for it. Big 3 cues apply here.

🎯

Foundational

Scapula Push-Ups

3 sets · Log reps per set

Cue: Push-up position, arms locked out. Without bending the elbows, let your chest sink between your shoulder blades (retraction), then push the ground away as hard as you can, rounding the upper back (protraction). Pure scapular movement -- serratus anterior drives the protraction. Ribs down, core braced. If your low back sags, the scapula can't move cleanly.

▶ Watch video

Prone Shoulder CARS (Handcuff Position)

3 sets each arm

Cue: Face down, start with hands behind the back like handcuffs. Sweep the arm through a full shoulder ROM -- out to the side, overhead into the Y position, and back. Emphasize HEIGHT off the ground at every position in the arc. Use objects (water bottles in the overhead position) to create accountability -- you must clear them. Slow, controlled, max range. Cramping is normal at the edges. Ribs stay in contact with the floor. Sub-max breathing.

▶ Watch video

Band External Rotation at 0 Degrees

2-3 sets · 15 reps each arm

Cue: Elbow pinned to side at 90 degrees. Rotate forearm outward against the band. Wrist neutral, squeeze shoulder blade back slightly. Control the return -- don't let the band snap it back. Ribs buttoned down -- if you flare, the scapula loses its seat on the rib cage and the shoulder takes unnecessary stress. Can be done on one leg for balance integration.

▶ Watch video

Band Internal Rotation

2-3 sets · 15 reps each arm

Cue: Band anchored at elbow height. Elbow at 90 pinned to side. Rotate forearm inward across your body. Control the eccentric. Throwers are ER-dominant, but the subscapularis is the most commonly injured cuff muscle in throwing athletes. Balance matters. Ribs down, brace engaged.

▶ Watch video

Band Pull-Aparts

2-3 sets · 15-20 reps

Cue: Light band at shoulder height, arms straight. Pull apart by squeezing shoulder blades together until band touches chest. Ribs DOWN -- don't arch your back to finish the rep. The rib cage position is what lets the scapula retract cleanly. Try these on one leg to build balance while you work the posterior chain. Initiate from the brace, from the breath out.

▶ Watch video

Prone Y-T-W-I Raises

2 sets · 8-10 reps each position · Bodyweight or 2-3 lb DBs

Cue: Face down, chin tucked. Y -- arms at 120 degrees, thumbs up, squeeze lower traps. T -- arms straight out, thumbs up, pinch shoulder blades. W -- elbows bent at 90, pull toward hips, externally rotate hands to ceiling. I -- arms return to anatomical position by your sides, palms forward, lift and squeeze posterior delts. Hold each 2-3 seconds. No shrugging. Ribs stay in contact with the floor -- if you flare off the ground, the scapula isn't loading properly.

▶ Watch video

Wall Slides

2-3 sets · 10-12 reps

Cue: Face wall, forearms flat. Chin tucked, eyes level. Slide arms up while pressing into the wall and actively protracting shoulder blades. Feel the serratus anterior engage along the side of your rib cage. Control the descent. The serratus is critical for scapular upward rotation during throwing -- if it's weak, the shoulder compensates and breaks down.

▶ Watch video

90/90 External Rotation (Thrower's Position)

2-3 sets · 10-12 reps each arm · Band or light DB

Cue: Arm at 90 degrees abduction, elbow at 90 -- the cocking phase position. Rotate forearm back and up, return with control. Shoulder blade pinched back. This is the most sport-specific cuff position for a QB. Ribs buttoned down throughout -- if the cage flares, the scapula tilts anteriorly and the rotator cuff gets impinged. Brace first, then move the arm.

Advanced

Crawl Position Sit-Outs

3 sets · Log reps per set (alternating sides)

Cue: Start in bear crawl position, knees 1 inch off ground. Lift opposite hand and foot off the ground. Thread the free leg underneath your body toward a nearly seated position -- but never touch the ground with your butt. Move controlled, return to crawl position before switching sides. This demands shoulder stability under dynamic load while the body rotates underneath. Ribs down, breathe through the movement. Initiate from the core out.

▶ Watch video

Band ER/IR at 90/90 with Rhythmic Stabilization

2-3 sets · 20-30s each arm

Cue: Assume the 90/90 throwing position. Hold a ball or light weight. A partner applies quick, unpredictable pushes in various directions. Resist and maintain position. This trains the reflexive stabilization that protects the shoulder during the violent acceleration and deceleration of throwing. Rib cage locked down -- the brace is what lets the shoulder absorb chaos without breaking.

▶ Watch video

Kneeling Backward Plyo Ball Throws

2 sets · 15-20 reps · 1-2 lb plyo ball

Cue: Kneeling facing away from a wall. Throw the 1-2 lb ball backward over your head into the wall. The key is the rapid eccentric loading -- catch and immediately throw again. This trains the posterior rotator cuff to handle deceleration forces. Keep the rib cage locked down and brace engaged. If your back arches on the throw, you've lost position. Sub-max breathing rhythm between throws.

▶ Watch video

Turkish Get-Up

2-3 reps per side · Start with no weight, then shoe on fist, then light KB

Cue: The arm stays vertical and packed -- shoulder blade pulled down and back into the socket -- throughout the entire movement from lying to standing. Each position is a checkpoint: pause, breathe, own it, then move. Eyes on the bell until standing. If one rep gets sloppy, the weight is too heavy. Rib cage position is critical at every transition -- flare and you lose the shoulder. This is the gold standard of integrated shoulder stability.
